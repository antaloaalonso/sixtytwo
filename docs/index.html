Running LLMs on your own GPUs shouldn't require a dozen tools and months of glue work.

Companies running models on their own hardware are stuck managing a mess of disconnected tools—DCGM for metrics, Slurm or K8s for scheduling, vLLM for serving, Grafana for dashboards, and a pile of scripts holding it all together. It takes months to get a model into production this way, and when something breaks, nobody knows which layer caused it.

We're building one platform that handles model deployment, GPU scheduling, and monitoring together. It understands LLM workloads natively—VRAM requirements, KV-cache sizing, which models can share hardware—so teams stop fighting over GPUs and start shipping.

Everything runs on your infrastructure. No telemetry leaves your network. No SaaS dependency. Works whether you're on Kubernetes, Slurm, or bare metal.

Open-source models are getting good enough to run on-prem. Data sovereignty regulations are tightening. The shift is happening—but the infrastructure layer between "we bought GPUs" and "we're serving models in production" doesn't exist yet.

We are a small team of engineers who have operated GPU infrastructure at scale. We know what breaks, what's missing, and what needs to exist.

One platform. Your hardware. Models in production.
