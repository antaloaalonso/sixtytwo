Sixtytwo

Reliability infrastructure for on-prem AI training.

When a GPU dies three days into a two-week run, the job should recover instead of restart.

Sonnet Xu Antonio Alonso-Stepanov Jessie Dong
What breaks today

On-prem teams still do training reliability by hand.

  • A single unhealthy node can kill a multi-day training run.
  • Engineers debug across PyTorch, Slurm, Kubernetes, DCGM, and custom scripts.
  • Cloud platforms absorb this operational work. On-prem stacks do not.
Problem
Training workloads fail for infrastructure reasons, not model reasons.

What on-prem training teams are dealing with now

01

Long-running jobs

Training runs last for days or weeks. One bad GPU, storage path, or network issue can wipe out expensive compute.

02

Fragmented tooling

The stack spans frameworks, schedulers, metrics, and infra tools that were not built to recover one workflow together.

03

Manual recovery

When something goes wrong, engineers inspect logs, replace nodes, restart jobs, and lose utilization while the cluster waits.

Typical stack today
PyTorch Ray Slurm Kubernetes NVIDIA DCGM Prometheus OpenTelemetry Internal scripts
Product
Monitor the training stack and automate recovery.

Sixtytwo handles the failure path.

01

Detect

Watch GPU telemetry, scheduler state, framework health, and checkpoint status.

02

Checkpoint

Capture model state before the failure cascades across the run.

03

Replace

Quarantine the bad machine and swap in healthy cluster capacity.

04

Resume

Restart the job from a known good point instead of from scratch.

Core product
  • Training-aware observability across the full stack.
  • Failure detection tied to automated recovery actions.
  • Integrations with training frameworks and cluster schedulers.
Initial integrations from the application

Why now
The workload is moving on-prem before the tooling exists.

Why this category opens now

More owned clusters

At sustained utilization, companies increasingly want to own the hardware instead of paying cloud margins forever.

More custom training

Open weights and proprietary data push teams to train, fine-tune, and distill models in private environments.

Failure cost is rising

The bigger and longer the run, the more expensive each avoidable infrastructure failure becomes.

Tooling gap is still open

Observability tools exist, but they are not built to coordinate checkpointing, node replacement, and resume for AI training.

Market
Enterprise software sold to infra and platform teams running training clusters.

Target customers and pricing scale with GPU count

Who buys
  • AI companies training large models on owned GPU clusters.
  • Enterprises moving sensitive training workloads off the public cloud.
  • Infrastructure and platform teams responsible for cluster uptime and utilization.
