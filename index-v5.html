Our Solution

Self-healing
training infrastructure

Sixtytwo monitors every layer of the training stack—hardware, network, framework—and automatically recovers from failures. No pager. No manual restarts. No lost compute.

01

Detect

Continuous health monitoring across GPU, NIC, NVLink, NCCL. Sub-second anomaly detection catches failures before runs crash.

02

Checkpoint

Automatically saves training state when issues are detected. No progress lost beyond the last few steps.

03

Replace

Quarantines the failing node and provisions a healthy spare from the pool. Handles all topology and networking reconfiguration.

04

Resume

Restores from checkpoint on the new topology and resumes training. The entire recovery takes minutes, not days.

Why now

The shift to
on-prem is real

Training in the cloud was the default. Now, the economics, regulations, and open-source ecosystem are pushing serious AI teams to own their compute.

1

Data sovereignty

Enterprises need full control over training data. Sending proprietary datasets to cloud providers creates unacceptable risk for regulated industries.

2

Cost at scale

At 1,000+ GPUs running 24/7, owning hardware pays for itself in 12–18 months. Cloud costs compound; capex depreciates.

3

Regulation

EU AI Act and emerging US policy require data residency and auditability. On-prem gives compliance by default.

4

Open-source models

Llama, Mistral, and others mean companies can pretrain and fine-tune their own models. The workloads are here—the infrastructure isn't.
