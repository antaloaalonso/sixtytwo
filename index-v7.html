Our thesis

A growing number of companies are pretraining their own models.

Data control and GPU economics are pushing companies on-prem. But going on-prem means taking on everything a cloud provider does invisibly — with none of their tooling.

Our solution

Self-healing
training infrastructure

Sixtytwo monitors every layer of the training stack — hardware, network, framework — and automatically recovers from failures. No pager. No manual restarts.

01

Detect

Continuous health monitoring across GPU, NIC, NVLink, NCCL. Sub-second anomaly detection.

02

Checkpoint

Automatically saves training state when issues are detected. No progress lost.

03

Replace

Quarantines failing node, provisions a healthy spare. Handles topology reconfiguration.

04

Resume

Restores from checkpoint on new topology. Recovery in minutes, not days.

Why now

The shift to on-prem
is accelerating

79% of enterprises have already repatriated some AI workloads from public cloud. The infrastructure to operate those clusters reliably doesn't exist yet.

01

Data sovereignty

73% of enterprises cite data privacy as top AI risk. 120+ countries now have data protection laws, up from 76 in 2011.

02

Cost at scale

On-prem breaks even vs cloud in under 4 months at high utilization. 8–18x cheaper per token for sustained workloads.

03

Regulation

EU AI Act high-risk rules take effect Aug 2026. Emerging US and global policy requires data residency and auditability.

04

Open-source models

Llama, Mistral, DeepSeek mean companies can pretrain on their own data. The workloads are here — the tooling isn't.
