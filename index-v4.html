Sixtytwo is the operational intelligence layer for on-prem AI clusters. Start with reliability. Expand into the control plane.

Initial wedge

Problem
Going on-prem means owning the failure path.

The current stack sees the cluster in fragments. No one handles the failure path end to end.

On-prem teams stitch together DCGM, Slurm, Kubernetes, Prometheus, and internal scripts. When a node goes unhealthy, the operator still has to coordinate the response.

Health

Signals live in different tools. Hardware issues are often detected late or without enough context.

Coordination

Recovery spans scheduler state, checkpoints, fleet health, and workload state, but teams orchestrate it manually.

Utilization

Every slow diagnosis or restart wastes cluster time and makes owned infrastructure look worse than it should.

DCGM Slurm Kubernetes Prometheus Scripts
Product
Connect the stack. Automate the first recovery loop.

Sixtytwo sits above the raw tools and turns telemetry into actions.

What the product does
  • Observability across GPU health, scheduler state, workload signals, and cluster behavior.
  • Failure detection tied to quarantine, checkpoint, and rescheduling workflows.
  • An operator surface for understanding where capacity is leaking and what to do next.
Initial wedge
Long-running training jobs, where failures are expensive and operational pain is immediate.
Why now
Owned clusters are arriving before the operations layer does.

The market is moving on-prem faster than tooling is catching up.

Data control

Sensitive data and model behavior are harder to push into third-party APIs or shared cloud workflows.

Open weights

More companies can build differentiated models on their own infrastructure instead of staying fully dependent on vendors.

Cloud spend

Cluster sprawl

The stack keeps getting deeper, and generic monitoring still stops at visibility.
